Chapter 01

Origins & Early History

The Football Association is Born

1863

On October 26, 1863, representatives from London clubs gathered at the Freemasons' Tavern to establish the Football Association — the world's first football governing body. They codified the rules of the game, separating it from rugby forever.

The First International Match

1872

England vs. Scotland. November 30, 1872. The first official international football match ended 0–0 in front of 4,000 spectators in Glasgow. A milestone that would spark a global rivalry lasting over 150 years.

World's First Football League

1888

The Football League was founded in England with just 12 clubs. It was the world's first professional football league — a model that every nation would eventually copy. Aston Villa won the inaugural title.

FIFA is Founded

1904

Fédération Internationale de Football Association (FIFA) was established in Paris on May 21, 1904, with just 7 founding member nations. Today it has 211 member associations — more than the United Nations.

Chapter 02

The FIFA World Cup

The greatest sporting event on Earth — held every four years since 1930, capturing the attention of billions.

1930

The First World Cup

Uruguay hosted — and won — the inaugural World Cup. Only 13 teams participated. European teams had to travel by boat for weeks to get there. Uruguay beat Argentina 4–2 in the final.

1950

The Maracanazo

In front of over 200,000 fans at the Maracanã, Uruguay stunned Brazil in the deciding match. No final was played — it was a round-robin. The silence of 200,000 Brazilians is said to have been deafening.

1954

Miracle of Bern

West Germany beat the heavily favoured Hungary 3–2. Hungary had been unbeaten for 4 years, including a 6–3 humiliation of England at Wembley. Germany's win remains one of sport's greatest upsets.

1958

Pelé at 17

A 17-year-old Pelé burst onto the world stage in Sweden, scoring 6 goals including a hat-trick in the semi-final. He remains the youngest player ever to score in a World Cup final.

1966

The Stolen Trophy

The Jules Rimet Trophy was stolen before the England-hosted tournament — and later found by a dog named Pickles. England went on to win their only World Cup, beating West Germany 4–2 in the final.

1970

Brazil's Greatest Team

Brazil won their third World Cup in Mexico, winning the Jules Rimet Trophy permanently. Their 1970 side — featuring Pelé, Jairzinho, Rivelino and Tostão — is widely considered the greatest team ever assembled.

1986

Maradona's Tournament

Diego Maradona almost single-handedly won the World Cup for Argentina. In the same quarter-final against England, he scored the "Hand of God" — then the "Goal of the Century", considered the greatest goal in history.

2006

Zidane's Last Act

The 2006 final between France and Italy went to extra time. With minutes left, the legendary Zinedine Zidane headbutted Marco Materazzi and was sent off. Italy won on penalties. A surreal end to a brilliant career.

2022

Messi's Redemption

In Qatar, Lionel Messi finally claimed the one trophy missing from his collection. Argentina beat France 4–2 on penalties after an extraordinary 3–3 draw — widely called the greatest World Cup final ever played.

Chapter 03

UEFA Champions League

Europe's elite club competition — the pinnacle of club football since 1955.

1955–56

The First European Cup

Real Madrid beat Reims 4–3 in the first-ever European Cup final in Paris. The competition was the vision of French journalist Gabriel Hanot — and Real Madrid would go on to dominate it completely.

Real Madrid

The Undisputed Kings

Real Madrid have won the competition a record 15 times, including the first 5 consecutive editions (1956–1960). No other club comes close — AC Milan is joint second with 7 titles.

2017

Remontada

FC Barcelona made a historic comeback against Paris Saint-Germain in the round of 16. After losing 4–0 in the first leg, Barça won 6–1 at home, scoring the final two goals in the 91st and the 95th minute to go through 6–5 on aggregate.

1999

Manchester United's Miracle

Manchester United trailed Bayern Munich 1–0 going into stoppage time of the final. Then Teddy Sheringham scored. Then Ole Gunnar Solskjær scored. United won 2–1 in the most dramatic finish in the final's history.

2005

The Miracle of Istanbul

AC Milan led Liverpool 3–0 at half-time of the final. In the second half, Liverpool scored three goals in six minutes to draw level, then won on penalties. Steven Gerrard led one of sport's greatest comebacks.

Ronaldo

The Record Scorer

Cristiano Ronaldo is the Champions League's all-time top scorer with over 140 goals — a record that may never be broken. He won the competition five times across two different clubs.

Chapter 05

Iconic Moments & Records

Record

Biggest International Win

Australia 31–0 American Samoa (2001) — the most one-sided international match ever played. Australia's Archie Thompson scored 13 goals in a single game, another record that still stands.

Upset

North Korea 1–0 Italy, 1966

Considered one of the biggest upsets in World Cup history. A part-time North Korean team eliminated four-time world champions Italy at the group stage. Italian players were pelted with tomatoes upon returning home.

Miracle

Leicester City, 2015–16

5,000 to 1. That was the odds on Leicester City winning the Premier League at the start of the season. They did it anyway — widely called the greatest sporting miracle in modern history.

Drama

Agüero's Last-Gasp Title

On the final day of the 2011–12 Premier League season, Manchester City needed to beat QPR. They were losing 2–1 in stoppage time. Then Džeko scored. Then Agüero. 3–2. City's first title in 44 years.

Upset

Greece — Euro 2004 Champions

Greece entered Euro 2004 as 150–1 outsiders. They beat Portugal twice — including in the final — playing one of the most disciplined defensive tournaments ever seen.

Record

The Goalkeeper Who Scores

Brazilian goalkeeper Rogério Ceni retired with over 130 goals — more than most strikers. Playing for São Paulo his entire career, he specialised in free kicks and penalties.

Tragedy

The Munich Air Disaster, 1958

On 6 February 1958, a plane carrying Manchester United's squad crashed on takeoff in Munich. Eight players died. Manager Matt Busby survived and rebuilt the club — winning the European Cup exactly 10 years later.

Bizarre

Fastest Red Card Ever

A professional footballer received a red card just 2 seconds after kick-off in a 2000 English match — the fastest dismissal in recorded football history.

Tragedy

Hilsborough Disaster, 1989

On 15 April 1989, during the FA Cup semi-final between Liverpool and Nottingham Forest, an overcrowding occurred in the Leppings Lane end of Hillsborough Stadium which crushed the supporters, resulting in the deaths of 97 Liverpool fans.

Did You Know?

Football Facts

FIFA has more member nations (211) than the United Nations (193).
The 1930 World Cup final used a different ball for each half — each team brought their own.
Brazil's yellow kit only became iconic because they won 3 World Cups in it and kept the Jules Rimet Trophy permanently.
Real Madrid won the first 5 European Cups — a streak that lasted from 1956 to 1960.
The Premier League was founded in 1992 and is now the most-watched sports league in the world.
Johan Cruyff's "Cruyff Turn" — invented in 1974 — is still one of the most used dribbling moves today.
Pelé is the only player ever to win 3 World Cups. No one else has won more than 2.
The Champions League anthem is based on a 1727 composition by George Frideric Handel.
